Berat

Berat, known as the “City of a Thousand Windows,” is one of Albania’s most important historical and cultural destinations. With a history spanning more than two millennia, the city is famous for its well-preserved Ottoman architecture, stone houses stacked along the hillside, and its unique harmony between nature and human settlement.

Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Berat offers visitors a journey through time, where ancient castles, Byzantine churches, and traditional neighborhoods coexist. Walking through Berat feels like exploring an open-air museum, making it a must-visit destination for history lovers and cultural travelers.

Main Attractions

Berat Castle

Berat Castle is a massive stone fortress that dominates the city skyline. Dating back over 2,400 years, it is still inhabited today and contains ancient churches, museums, and breathtaking views over the Osum River and old town below.

Ethnographic Museum

Located in a traditional Ottoman-era house, this museum offers insight into daily life in Berat during the 18th and 19th centuries, showcasing clothing, household tools, and traditional craftsmanship.

Gorica Bridge

This elegant Ottoman bridge connects the historic Mangalem and Gorica quarters. It is one of the most photographed landmarks in Berat and a perfect spot for an evening walk.

Recommended Restaurants

Homemade Food Lili

A family-run restaurant offering authentic Albanian cuisine prepared with local ingredients. Known for its warm hospitality and traditional recipes passed down through generations.

Antigoni Restaurant

Set above the Osum River, Antigoni offers traditional Albanian dishes paired with scenic views, making it a favorite for both locals and visitors.
