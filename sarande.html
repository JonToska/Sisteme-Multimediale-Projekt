Sarandë

Sarandë is one of Albania’s most popular seaside destinations, located along the stunning Ionian coast in the south of the country. Known for its crystal-clear waters, sunny climate, and relaxed atmosphere, the city attracts visitors from all over Europe, especially during the summer months.

Beyond its beaches, Sarandë is a gateway to some of Albania’s most significant natural and historical attractions, including ancient ruins, national parks, and pristine coastal villages. The combination of natural beauty, rich history, and modern tourism infrastructure makes Sarandë an ideal destination for both relaxation and exploration.

Main Attractions

Ksamil Islands

Ksamil is a small coastal village near Sarandë, famous for its white sandy beaches and clear turquoise waters. It features several small islands just offshore, calm seas ideal for swimming, and beautiful views that make it one of the most visited seaside destinations in southern Albania.

Butrint National Park

Butrint is an ancient archaeological site surrounded by nature, located just south of Sarandë near the Greek border. With ruins dating back over 2,500 years, it includes a Roman theater, city walls, temples, and offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.

The Blue Eye (Syri i Kaltër)

The Blue Eye is a stunning natural spring located near Sarandë, famous for its deep blue color and crystal-clear water. Surrounded by lush greenery, it is one of Albania’s most beautiful natural landmarks and a popular spot for visitors seeking peaceful scenery and unique photography opportunities.

Recommended Restaurants

Haxhi

One of Sarandë’s most popular seafood restaurants, offering freshly caught fish and traditional coastal dishes.

Fish Filipi

A cozy seaside taverna known for grilled seafood and relaxed atmosphere, perfect after a day at the beach.
